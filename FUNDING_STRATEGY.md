# TowerGuard — Funding Strategy

## Positioning

We are NOT pitching a drone startup.

We ARE pitching:

> **Autonomous perimeter intelligence for persistent security — commercial-proven, government-ready**

This framing matters in every conversation. "Drone startup" triggers commodity thinking and hardware-cost objections. "Perimeter intelligence platform" triggers security budget conversations and software-multiple valuations.

Critically: **TowerGuard is not a GovCon-only product.** The civilian commercial market — farms, warehouses, construction sites, office parks, property owners — is the primary revenue path and the proof-of-concept story that makes every government pitch credible. A DIU or AFWERX reviewer who sees real commercial deployments takes the pitch seriously in a way they won't for a paper prototype.

**Dual-market strategy:**
- Commercial sales → early revenue, real-world data, pilot sites, case studies
- Government funding → non-dilutive capital, enterprise scale, defensible moat

Neither track should be designed to exclude the other. Compliance overhead (FedRAMP, ITAR, CMMC) gets bolted on when the contract value justifies it — not day one.

## Track 1 — Commercial Revenue

This happens before any government contract closes.

### Target Buyers

| Segment | Why They Buy |
|---|---|
| Large property / ranch owners | Acres of terrain, no affordable 24/7 solution exists |
| Warehouse + distribution | After-hours theft and perimeter breach is a real P&L line item |
| Construction sites | Equipment theft costs the industry ~$1B/year. Active sites are soft targets. |
| Office parks / commercial campuses | Replace or augment human patrol guards — lower cost, always on |
| Private security firms | White-label or resell as a premium managed service offering |

### Go-to-Market (Commercial)
- Direct outreach to property managers and security directors
- Private security firm partnership (they have the client relationships)
- Initial pricing: SaaS subscription per nest + optional managed service tier
- Pilot program: 30-day free trial with hardware loaner (Phase 2)

## Track 2 — Non-Dilutive Government Funding

Non-dilutive first. We build technical credibility before touching equity.

### AFWERX (Air Force)

**Why:** Largest defense innovation program by volume. Open topic areas include autonomous systems, base security, and persistent ISR.

**Fit:** TowerGuard directly addresses base perimeter security — a known Air Force pain point with active procurement interest.

**Path:**
1. Submit under AFWERX Open Topic (no specific topic required)
2. Pitch as "persistent autonomous perimeter awareness for installation security"
3. Phase 1: $50K–$250K feasibility study
4. Phase 2: Up to $1.5M prototype development

**Timeline:** AFWERX accepts applications on a rolling basis. Aim for submission within 60 days of Phase 1 completion.

---

### SBIR — Small Business Innovation Research

**Why:** Federal program across all major agencies (DoD, DHS, DoE, NASA). Non-dilutive. Designed for pre-revenue tech companies.

**Fit:** TowerGuard fits multiple open SBIR topic areas:
- DoD: Autonomous base defense systems
- DHS: Critical infrastructure protection technology
- Army: Counter-UAS + perimeter security (dual use)

**Path:**
1. Identify specific open topic in current solicitation cycle
2. Submit Phase I (6 months, up to $275K depending on agency)
3. Phase I deliverable: feasibility study + prototype demo (TowerGuard Sim qualifies)
4. Phase II: up to $1.75M for prototype development

**Key requirement:** Small business (<500 employees), US-owned, primary performance in US.

---

### DIU — Defense Innovation Unit

**Why:** Rapid commercial technology adoption for DoD. Shorter contract cycles than traditional procurement. Actively looking for dual-use commercial tech.

**Fit:** TowerGuard as commercial perimeter intelligence platform with clear DoD application.

**Path:** DIU Commercial Solutions Opening (CSO). Company must have commercial traction or credible prototype. Phase 1 sim + pilot site data is the minimum bar.

**Timeline:** Phase 2 target — after pilot deployment.

---

### DHS — Dept. of Homeland Security

**Why:** Critical infrastructure protection is a DHS mandate. Power plants, water treatment, border installations are all active procurement areas.

**Fit:** TowerGuard nest deployment at critical infrastructure sites.

**Path:** DHS SBIR program + direct S&T Directorate engagement.

---

### Army xTech

**Why:** Army-specific competition for early-stage defense tech. Prize money + transition pathway.

**Fit:** Perimeter security, force protection, autonomous systems.

**Path:** Annual competition. Submit white paper. If selected, present at competition event. Prize: $250K + potential Army contract transition.

## Funding Narrative Structure

### Commercial Pitch (property owners, warehouses, construction, security firms)

**1. Problem** — Your perimeter has gaps. Cameras miss blind spots. Guards get tired. Single-drone systems go offline to charge. Nothing watches continuously.

**2. Solution** — TowerGuard deploys a nest of 3–4 drones in rotation. One is always airborne. The platform flags anomalies. You decide what to do about it.

**3. Proof** — Working simulation now. Hardware pilot within 60 days.

**4. Ask** — Pilot site agreement. 30 days, your property, your conditions.

---

### Government / GovCon Pitch (SBIR, AFWERX, DHS, DIU)

**1. Problem** — Human endurance limits and single-drone downtime gaps leave perimeters vulnerable. This is a solved problem operationally — rotating guards, overlapping patrols — but unsolved technologically at scale.

**2. Solution** — Persistent drone rotation from an autonomous nest. The perimeter is always watched.

**3. Differentiation** — Platform-first architecture. Sensor-agnostic. Scales from 1 nest to 50. Commercial deployments already running.

**4. Founder advantage** — 20-year Army veteran with direct tower guard experience. Built by someone who lived the problem.

**5. Technical credibility** — Working simulation, defined architecture, commercial pilot data, documented integration path.

**6. Ask** — Phase I feasibility funding to complete hardware integration and additional pilot deployments.

## What We Are NOT Doing

- No equity raise until non-dilutive path is exhausted or a strategic acquirer emerges
- No VC pitch decks until we have pilot data
- No hardware investment before sim validation
- No "drone company" positioning in any document
