# Call w/ Kyle McKuhen — Skyline/Hammerhead (MPP Mentor)

**Goal of the call:** Leave with (1) clarity on what a mentor-protégé relationship with them actually looks like, (2) the fastest path to first GovCon revenue, and (3) a defined next step.

**Posture:** Listen 70 / talk 30. You're being evaluated for coachability, not pitching for funding.

---

## The 5 that matter most (ask these no matter what)

1. **"What does the mentor-protégé structure look like on your side — are we talking a formal SBA MPP agreement with a JV, or informal mentorship first?"**
   *Why: Formal SBA MPP lets a JV bid SDVOSB set-asides using THEIR past performance — that's the single biggest door your certification opens. Informal mentorship is fine as a start, but know which game you're in.*

2. **"What contract vehicles and prime relationships does Skyline/Hammerhead hold, and where do you have subcontracting needs I could fill today?"**
   *Why: Subbing on their existing work (IT modernization, cyber, AI — your cap statement core) is almost certainly your fastest first dollar. Faster than SBIR, faster than TowerGuard.*

3. **"If you were me — SDVOSB, cleared-eligible, 20 years Army IT, zero contract history — what's my first 12 months? Services subcontracting first, or push SBIR/AFWERX for the product?"**
   *Why: This is the strategic fork. Let him make the case; his answer tells you how he thinks and what he'd actually use you for.*

4. **"What do you expect from a protégé? What separates the ones that work from the ones that waste your time?"**
   *Why: Coachability signal. Also surfaces his real motivation — mentors in SBA MPP can take up to 40% equity in the protégé JV and get subcontracting/small-business-credit benefits. Know what he's getting out of it.*

5. **"What's the right next step if this conversation goes well — and what do you need from me to get there?"**
   *Why: Never end a mentor call without a concrete deliverable and date.*

## Revenue path & mechanics

6. "Which agencies do you know best — where are your customer relationships strongest?" *(Their agency map = your target list.)*

7. "How did you build past performance from zero? Sub work first, or a small direct award — micro-purchase / SAP-level contracts?"

8. "Do you see SDVOSB sole-source awards actually happening in your space, or is it all competed set-asides?" *(VA is the SDVOSB stronghold — Vets First. If they touch VA, that's a lane.)*

9. "Is there a task order or proposal right now where a cleared-eligible AI/cyber/network guy could plug in?" *(Direct, concrete, makes it real.)*

## TowerGuard-specific (only if the conversation opens the door)

10. "Do you have customers in force protection, physical security, or installation management I should understand better before pushing TowerGuard?"

11. "Any experience with AFWERX or DoD SBIR? Worth my time now, or premature before pilot data?"

12. "Blue UAS compliance — dealbreaker from day one for anything DoD-adjacent, or only at contract stage?"

## Logistics & candor items (raise it before he discovers it)

13. **"One thing you should know: I'm currently based in the Philippines, relocating as needed. How much of a problem is that for the work you'd route to me — and for SBIR primary-performance requirements?"**
    *Why: He WILL find out. Raising it yourself converts a red flag into a trust signal. Have your answer ready: US citizen, TX LLC, willing to relocate/travel for contract work.*

14. "Who else should I be talking to — OSDBU small business offices, APEX Accelerator, matchmaking events worth attending?"

## Prep notes

- **Have ready to send during the call:** capability statement (Jul 2026 rev) + TowerGuard one-pager.
- **Don't:** demo TowerGuard unprompted, ask him to invest, or mention other ventures (Kairos etc.). One story: SDVOSB IT/AI firm + one product bet.
